Election Commission of India

The Election Commission of India (ECI) is an autonomous constitutional authority responsible for administering election processes in India. It was established on 25th January 1950, shortly after India's independence.

The ECI operates under the provisions of the Constitution of India and oversees the conduct of elections to the Parliament, State Legislatures, and the offices of the President and Vice President of India.

Over the years, the Election Commission has played a crucial role in ensuring free, fair, and transparent elections in India, upholding the democratic principles enshrined in the Constitution.

For more information, you can visit the official website of the Election Commission of India.
